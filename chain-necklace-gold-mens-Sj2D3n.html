Understanding the Nuances of Precious Metals and Stones

For many, the allure of men and necklaces lies in the classic appeal of precious metals. Gold is measured in karats (k), with 24k being pure gold. 14k and 18k gold are more common for jewelry as they are mixed with other alloys for added strength. Sterling silver, marked as 925, consists of 92.5% pure silver and is a timeless choice for its bright luster. Platinum is the most durable and rarest of the precious metals, offering a sophisticated, understated elegance. When stones are involved, understanding the 4Cs—cut, color, clarity, and carat—is essential to judging their quality.
